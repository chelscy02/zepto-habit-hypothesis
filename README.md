## Overview

**The Habit Hypothesis** is an independent business research project that investigates one central question:

> **Did Zepto build genuine customer habits, or did it simply buy temporary growth through discounts?**

Instead of treating Zepto's rapid growth as either a success story or a failure, this project examines audited financial data, industry reports, and original customer research to evaluate whether the company's economics are becoming structurally stronger.

The project combines financial analysis, product thinking, customer behaviour analysis, and strategic reasoning into an interactive presentation.

---

## Research Questions

This investigation explores five key questions:

- Can Zepto acquire customers without permanently relying on discounts?
- Do customers become habitual users?
- Can advertising become a meaningful profit engine?
- Can profitability arrive before capital runs out?
- Under what conditions would this entire thesis be wrong?

---

## Methodology

This research combines multiple sources of evidence including:

- Zepto DRHP (Primary Source)
- Blinkit and Swiggy public filings
- Industry reports
- Original qualitative analysis of 150+ customer reviews
- Independent financial modelling
- Competitive benchmarking

Every assumption has been explicitly labelled throughout the project.

---

## Key Findings

Some major insights from the research include:

- 91% reduction in digital marketing cost per order
- Advertising revenue emerging as an important monetisation lever
- Evidence of increasing customer habit formation
- Profitability remains uncertain despite improving operating metrics
- Final conclusion depends on long-term cohort retention that is not yet publicly available
